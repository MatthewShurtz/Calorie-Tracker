Calorie Tracker

A simple tool to help understand your eating habits.

Why So Simple?

Looking at the world of nutrutional science, it's easy to get overwhelmed and confused with the conflicting information. The case has been made by varying proponents to go low-carb, vegan, carnivore, low-fat, keto, Atkins, Mediterranean, vegetarian, ect. I want to tell you a little secret that really isn't a secret. You ready? They all work. Shocked? I was. I used to be a huge advocate of low-carb and in some degree I still am. But I have been more convince of the importance of knowing how much food you eat.

The research, at least in my opinion, has shown that it doesn't really matter what you eat but rather how much you eat. It doesn't matter if you are low-carb or low-fat. If this is true, then the only number we should concern ourselves with is the number of total calories we consume.

I understand if the idea of counting your calories is a chore but it can be really revealing as the amount of food you are eating. You can also identify some eating patterns. Do you eat more on the weekends? Do you have a small breakfast but a giant dinner? Do you snack a lot? Do you consume a lot calories through beavages? Did something happen this week that sent you into a food frenzy? All of these question can be answer simply through tracking. You are you own scientist and test subject. So how can this simple web app help? Good question

How to Use This App

This app does is add your total calories from the day. There are a couple ways you can maximize its use. One, you can write down everything you eat during the day and after you have had your last meal simple add the food and number of calories into the respective field. Two, you can write everything down you eat along with the number of calories then after your last meal you can add them to this app and it will calories. I would suggest the first method for a week just to get a sense of what you are eating. After that, you can choose either method. You can keep track through a few methods. You can use a piece of paper and a pen/pencil, you can enter everything on a note app on your phone, or you can use your calendar that way you can see how many calories you ate on a particular day.

For a quick reference on the number of calories in a food item, here is a couple of sources to help you figure that out. The first is a pdf. You hit the cmd+f/alt+f to bring up the find box. For example, when you pull up the pdf, hit the cmd+f/alt+f and type in apple. You will see "apple" is highlighted. Note that the portion size might need to be caluculated as well.

Another is Calorie King. Simple search for the food item and it will show you different portion sizes and the number of calories
